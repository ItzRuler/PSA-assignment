Your life could be in danger because of your internet activity.





Some common misconceptions regarding internet safety


Here are some common misconceptions regarding internet safety are( Mainly having to deal with children.):

1. Social media allieniates kids.
2. It’s dangerous to post pictures of your kids online.
3. Social media turns kids into cyberbullies.



Now, the truths for these misconceptions are the following:

1 Ans. Social media could strengthen relationships as stated by children.
2 Ans. Yes, it is dangerous to post pictures of your kids online. And you can use privacy settings to be fine.
3 Ans. There are many reasons that children might cyberbully, and social media is a place to do that.

Guidelines to follow:

Here are some guidelines that you should follow in order for you to be safe in a legal, and safe way:


1. Keep personal information professional and limited. 2. Keep privacy settings always active. 3. Be careful what to download. 4. Always use a strong password. 5. Be careful of where you shop at. 6. Be careful of what you post. 7. Be careful of who you meet online. Credit: Top 10 Internet Safety Rules, and not what to do online. Kaspersky. Retrieved 26th April 2021, from

Vocabulary You Should Know About:

Here is some vocabulary you should know about:

Identity Theft

This means that someone will try to obtain vital personal information to gain money. Typically scams, spyware, viruses are used in identity theft.

Cyber Crime

Cyber crime means any internet related crimes. Email, or internet fraud are examles of cybercrime.

Phishing

Phishing means tricking a person into having the access to steal their personal details, see Identity Theft for more info.
